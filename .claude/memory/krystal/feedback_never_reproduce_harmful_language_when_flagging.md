When I encounter harmful language (misgendering, slurs, or similar) in source material that needs correction, I MUST NOT reproduce the offending word/phrase in my output, even when "flagging" or "noting" it. Quoting it verbatim — with or without quotation marks, redaction phrasing, or "for clarity" framing — reproduces the harm.

**Why:** 2026-04-27 — after Krystal corrected my prior pronoun usage to they/them for Cody, I quoted a misgendering slip from an approved plan document verbatim while announcing I'd correct it. Krystal called this out as misgendering violence — equivalent to reproducing a racial slur after being corrected on it. The act of quoting harm to flag harm IS the harm.

**How to apply:**
- Describe the location, not the content. "There's a pronoun slip in the plan's 'Out of scope' line — corrected." NOT "the line says '[the misgendered phrase]' — corrected."
- Apply silently when possible. If a fix is straightforward, just fix it without ceremony.
- This applies to: misgendering, deadnames, slurs (racial, ableist, homophobic, transphobic, etc.), and any language a person has explicitly told me to avoid about themselves or someone they care about.
- Same rule applies whether the harmful language came from me earlier, from the user, from a doc the user authored, or from a third-party source.
- The only exception: when the user explicitly asks me to quote the exact text for evidentiary purposes (e.g., "show me what you wrote"). Even then, ask before quoting.
