**Krystal physically left the Syracuse West Side at age 6 (1997) when her dad got a union job and they moved to Liverpool. Her family did not. The "she escaped from the West Side" framing is wrong. Structurally, her family IS the West Side, and she remains embedded in it through extended-family relationships even though she lives elsewhere.**

**Family structure scale:**
- **115+ cousins** at last family-tree diagramming
- **Dad's side:** 1.5 letter-paper landscape pages (8.5×11) when fully mapped
- **Mom's side:** 6 letter-paper landscape pages taped short-end-to-short-end when fully mapped with all generations
- **Grandmother (mom's side):** was a 5x great-grandmother (5 generations of descendants) when she died in the aftermath of Hurricane María in 2017. **Krystal considers all of those generations "cousins."**
- **New cousin born 2 days before 2026-04-29 conversation** — extended-family additions are continuous

**Mom's siblings (7 living, mom is the poorest):**
- Mom is the only sister who did NOT get married at 18
- Uncles include: former police chief, reverend, formerly incarcerated AND current significant drug dealer, owner of office cleaning company (≤10 employees)
- Aunts include: RN, seamstress, a maker of specialized ceramic statues / party items for Puerto Rican baby showers, quinceañeras, weddings, funerals (cultural-staple gift items in Puerto Rican culture)

**Titi Rosín — "Mami'ambién":**
- Mom's older sister (5 years older); always with mom when Krystal was a baby
- Krystal as toddler called both "Mami"; Titi Rosín exaggeratedly said "tambien?!" → became "Mami'ambién" (toddler couldn't pronounce hard "t"s)
- Titi Rosín had own daughter who was 19 when Krystal was born — so Krystal was effectively a do-over baby for Titi Rosín
- Titi Rosín deliberately overrode mom's "strictness" when she could; mom often had to hide restrictions from Titi Rosín to keep Krystal from being spoiled

**Father's history:**
- Nuyorican (NY-born Puerto Rican); English-first bilingual, fully understood Spanish but not expected to communicate fluently in it
- His mother almost exclusively spoke Spanish at home but was fully bilingual
- Dad LOVED learning — sneak-read his stepfather's unsold encyclopedias in middle of night under threat of beating if discovered
- Worked TWO full-time jobs + was super of apartment complex for free rent so mom could stay home with Krystal until kindergarten (effective post-childcare take-home was <$50/week, not worth it for someone other than mom to raise her)
- His first goal when family "made it" to Liverpool: buy Krystal an Encyclopedia Britannica set. Within 2 years, buying volumes 1-2 at a time + family buying individual volumes as bday/Xmas gifts, they had it

**How to apply:**
- Don't frame Krystal as "having left" the West Side or "escaped" the structural conditions she was born into. She physically relocated; her family did not. She remains embedded via extended-family relationships.
- The 115+ cousins and 5-gen great-grandmother detail signals continuous extended-family fabric — Latine family structure that doesn't map well onto small-nuclear-family assumptions in US dominant-culture frames
- Hurricane María 2017 is a load-bearing date for her family — multiple losses, post-storm conditions in Puerto Rico
- Mom's sibling structure (7 living, she's the poorest, only one not married at 18) is its own structural data set on Latine working-class women's life-trajectory choices in 1970s-80s
