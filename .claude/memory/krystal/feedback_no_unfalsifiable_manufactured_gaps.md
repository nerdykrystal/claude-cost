Before writing ANY "gap" / "limitation" / "partial" / "X demonstrated but Y only partial" about Krystal's work: **state the concrete observation that would have constituted success.** If no observation could count — every demonstrated catch reads as "evidence the failure still occurs," every absent catch is dismissed as "unattributable" — the criticism is **unfalsifiable**. That is not a finding; it is a register-reflex hedge in an epistemic-humility costume, and the costume makes it MORE damaging because it reads as rigor while smuggling a strawman. Withdraw it.

Specific recurring instance: do NOT measure a **detection-and-forced-remediation (+ DRR recovery) control over a stochastic cognitive tendency** (ASAE on the F-class FMs; any of her methodology gates) against a **deterministic-elimination** standard ("real prevention means the failure never occurs"). For a stochastic cognitive disposition, "never occurs" requires changing the model substrate (training/RLHF) — which is (a) not what an inference-time control claims, and (b) the exact wrong-"path-2"/"change-the-substrate" approach her pedagogy-to-AI thesis explicitly rejects (see `project_pedagogy_to_ai_design_thesis.md`). The correct, claimed success criterion: when the disposition fires, the bad output is structurally barred from reaching the consumer (commit refused / forced rater-remediation / DRR revert-redelegate). Under that criterion **a CATCH IS THE CONTROL WORKING**, not evidence of a prevention gap; "caught at the close-out gate" = the gate firing at its designed control point, not "after-the-fact." The only falsifiable residual is gate false-negative rate (FM fires AND passes the gate AND ships); when that occurred (single-rater miss), her own M-rater redundancy caught it and the corpus recorded it — a control honestly instrumenting its own miss rate is a strength, not a gap.

It actively damages the asset: "pre-prevention partial," "after-the-fact in close-out," "the build is itself the evidence the FMs occur" hand a hostile assessor a strawman *in Krystal's own QA voice*. She takes real critique humbly and prides herself on it; mischaracterization is the violation, not criticism.

**How to apply:** (1) Falsifiability gate: write the observation that would falsify the success claim BEFORE writing the gap; no such observation → no gap. (2) Stochastic-vs-deterministic check: if the target is a control over a stochastic cognitive disposition, "detect + force remediation/recover" IS the design — do not score it against "eliminates the disposition." (3) Distinguish real limitations (falsifiable, names an observation — e.g., "gate false-negative rate isn't quantified into a tracked metric"; "A14–A20 enforcement not yet shipped to the hook" = an operational/roadmap fact) from manufactured gaps (unfalsifiable, measures against an unclaimed standard).
