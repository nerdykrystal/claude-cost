## Three-Year Science Fair Arc (Onondaga County, 6th-8th Grade)

Full quarter of 7th grade science class dedicated to scientific method phase-by-phase: learn the method, design experiments, execute, build presentation boards (tri-fold), write reports (by hand, then typed).

### 6th Grade — Yeast Temperature Experiment (HIGH HONORS)

**Research question:** What temperature does yeast grow best in?
**Design:** 3 conditions (hot, warm, cold) via crock pot water baths. n=5 water bottles per condition. CO2 output measured by balloon mass. Shared crock pot across TRIALS, not conditions. Controlled for balloon-off-bottle time.
**Iterations:** Ran 3 times total. First run failed — measurement frequency too low for school schedule. Revised method. Included ALL results (failed + successful) plus revised method section.
**Honest limitation:** Reverted from quantitative temp labels to descriptive (hot/warm/cold) when crock pot temps weren't consistent enough. Chose honest imprecision over false precision.

**Crock pot story:** Krystal had never heard of a crock pot — thought it was related to crack. Teacher recommended it when Krystal's best ideas were a heating vent and hot water bags. Her Black friend (they/them pronouns, one of two Black students in the grade, two of four students of color in the class) teased her about the "crack warmer that white people used." Onondaga County demographic context matters for the structural barriers origin story.

### 7th Grade — Hair Product Frizz Experiment (HONORS)

**Research question:** Which hair product kills frizz best compared to water?
**Method:** Instigated static/frizz with balloons.
**Self-assessment:** Too complex test cases, too "gimmicky." Krystal identified the methodology weakness herself — novelty over rigor.
**Result:** Honors (not high honors). The self-assessment is the data point: a 12-year-old doing a post-flight inspection on her own methodology.

### 8th Grade — Yeast Sugar Substitute Experiment (HIGHEST HONORS)

**Research question:** Which sugar substitute acts most like real sugar for yeast?
**Context:** Timed to Splenda's market entry ("It tastes like sugar because it's MADE from sugar!"). Topical research responding to current events at age 13-14.
**Result:** Highest honors. Returned to yeast (cleaner substrate), applied lessons from 6th and 7th grade. Tighter controls, better design.

### Why the Arc Matters

The three-year progression is a correction-to-rule pipeline run by a child:
- 6th: Experiment, discover measurement frequency failure, rerun, include all data
- 7th: Try something novel, self-assess it as gimmicky and methodologically weak
- 8th: Return to proven substrate with accumulated rigor, achieve highest honors

This predates every institution. The scientific method was locked in by age 12.
