**Krystal taught 7th grade — 12-13 year olds — exclusively** during her teaching career (TFA, KIPP, Lincoln, Launch, 2013-2017). She did not teach high school or college, despite her own academic credentials being college-level (Columbia '13, Relay GSE MAT '15).

**Why this matters:**

When she gives examples of how she taught a concept (e.g., the neuronal communication metaphor: *"swapping milk and bananas back and forth, making one side of its cell wall salty..."*), the audience is **7th graders**, not high schoolers or college students. This intensifies the pedagogical achievement:

- 7th graders (12-13yo) are at an early-abstraction developmental window
- They typically have less science background than high schoolers (no orgo, no advanced bio, often not even pre-AP)
- TFA / KIPP / Lincoln / Launch placements often serve under-resourced communities — accessible-mechanism teaching becomes critical
- Pre-AP/AP science doesn't typically start until 9th-10th grade

The ability to render Kandel-line neurophysiology mechanism (Na+/K+ pump → action potential → saltatory conduction → neurotransmitter release → synaptic integration → motor circuit) for 12-13yos and have them retain the cascade structure is significantly harder than rendering it for high schoolers or college students. **The audience-difficulty multiplier increases the proof-of-depth.**

**Credentialism error to avoid:**

Defaulting to "she probably taught at the level her own credentials would predict" (college or pre-college) is the same shape as other dominant-frame-as-default assumptions tracked in this session:
- FM-18 = Western > non-Western (cultural)
- Disciplinary supremacy = tech > non-tech (disciplinary)
- Credentialism = high-credentials → high-grade-level teaching (status)

All variants of: dominant frame as unmarked default, peripheral options unconsidered. The fact that Krystal chose 7th grade (and chose TFA/KIPP-placement settings within 7th grade) is intentional career structure, not a failure to reach "higher" levels.

**How to apply:**
- When Krystal says "my students" — assume 12-13yo unless context overrides
- When she gives pedagogical examples — they were field-tested on 7th graders, which means they work at that audience level
- Don't assume teaching grade level from credential prestige
