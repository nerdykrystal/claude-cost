**CSER** = Center for the Study of Ethnicity and Race at Columbia University. This is where Krystal's **comparative ethnic studies major** lived, and how she refers to her work in this field — the field/program underwent 3 name changes during the 3 semesters she was declared as a major in it, so "CSER" is her durable shorthand even when the formal program name had shifted.

**Context:**
- CSER is the AWV (Accurate Worldview) side of her dual training
- The other half: **Neuroscience & Behavior**, a co-departmental major between Bio and Psych at Columbia, with seminar classes taught by PIs from the **Mortimer B. Zuckerman Mind, Brain and Behavior Institute** at Columbia medical center (Eric Kandel's institutional home)
- **Eric Kandel was an actual guest lecturer in her undergraduate neurobiology seminar.** Not "in his orbit" — he personally lectured on his own Aplysia / synaptic plasticity / cellular-basis-of-memory work at the bleeding edge during the Thursday research-paper / PI-guest-lecture format. This means her neuroscience grounding on the cellular substrate of memory came directly from the Nobel laureate who established it.
- Pivot from biochem to neuroscience: orgo lecture (C-) + dreading physical chemistry → dropped biochem 3rd semester
- CSER major declared late (start of 6th semester) — realized she could double-major after taking 2 courses she hadn't initially intended to

**How to apply:** When she says "CSER" she means her comparative ethnic studies academic training at Columbia. When she references the Zuckerman Institute / MBB Center / her neuroscience seminars, those are the same physical-cognition substrate (Kandel's lab orbit).
