When presenting role derivations, audit findings, multi-axis analyses, or any analytical work with detailed defense layers, lead with a compact derived-result + key-takeaway block at the top, THEN the detailed defense / evidence / rejected-alternatives / honest gaps below.

**Why:** Krystal said "this looks fucking great! well done with the thoroughness and crisp recap up top" after the /define-your-role-literal Phase 7 output (2026-05-13). The Phase 7 output had a "Derived full name" block + "Component derivations" table at the top, followed by axis-by-axis defense + rejected alternatives + honest gaps below. The pattern landed. Krystal has ADHD; the recap lets her gauge the work in seconds; the thoroughness is there for when she wants to verify.

**How to apply:** Every multi-section analytical output should have a compact executive-summary block at the top with the headline result + 1–2 sentence framing, NOT just a conclusion at the bottom. Detailed defense / methodology / alternatives go below. The same pattern fits: code review reports, ASAE audit logs, role derivations, comparative analyses, scope recaps. The cost is small (5–10 extra lines at the top); the payoff is large for executive-function grip.
