## The rule (NYS General Business Law §130)

1. **Operating under literal legal name** (e.g., "Krystal Jazmin Martinez" or "Krystal Martinez" — exactly the proprietor's name with no additions): NO Certificate of Assumed Name (DBA) required.

2. **Operating under any name with descriptors / modifications** added: Certificate of Assumed Name required. This includes:
   - "[Last name] Tutoring" / "[Last name] Services" / "[Last name] Consulting"
   - "[Last name] Methods" / "[Last name] Systems"
   - "Krystal's [anything]" / "[Anything] Krystal"
   - Any name that adds words / suffixes / descriptors to the proprietor's legal name

3. **Filing location:** County Clerk's office in the county where business is conducted.

4. **Fee:** $25 in most NY counties; $100 in NYC five boroughs (NY County / Kings / Queens / Bronx / Richmond).

5. **Display requirement (§130(4)):** Certified copy of the filing must be displayed on the premises where the business is conducted under the assumed name.

6. **Surname restriction:** Sole proprietor business names CANNOT use words implying another person's surname (i.e., can't pretend to be a name you don't have).

## How to apply

1. **Resume verb selection** — apply the table above. "Founded" requires legal-entity verification. "Owner" requires legal-entity verification. "Operated" / "Self-employed" / "Author of" / "Consulting partnership" cover unverified-incorporation cases conservatively.

2. **Cover letters + tailored materials** — same constraints. Do NOT introduce "Founded" / "Owner" framings for entities not in the verified-LLC column above.

3. **LinkedIn / Indeed / Upwork profiles** — same constraints. The profile titles will likely default to single-line "Founder of X" framings; correct to the conservative framing where incorporation is unverified.

4. **When the underlying legal status changes** — if Krystal incorporates Stahl Systems or files DBAs for Martinez Methods / Martinez Tutoring, this memo becomes outdated. Re-check status before propagating verb upgrades.

5. **Cross-state operations** — Krystal has operated in NY / LA / Boston / etc. across her career. NYS §130 governs NY-conducted business. CA / MA / other states have their own DBA filing requirements. If a specific entity-state combination becomes load-bearing for a future application, research that state's filing rules separately.
