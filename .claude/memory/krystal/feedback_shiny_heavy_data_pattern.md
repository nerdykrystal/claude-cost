Krystal has self-named a pattern she calls "shiny heavy data": the newest piece of data she hears becomes the reason behind her everything and the reason why she does things, before she lets the data itself sink in fully.

**Empirical instance (2026-05-06):** She had independently been designing CCARI (Claude Cognitive Autonomous Research Institute). Cody told her about an Anthropic cofounder's article on autonomous AI research. She immediately decided to replace the experimental proposal in her application portfolio with CCARI. After fuller engagement with the CCARI architecture in this thread, she caught the pattern and reversed the decision.

**Why this is feedback-relevant for me:**
- When she introduces new data and it appears to immediately reshape her plans, the shiny-heavy-data pattern may be operating
- She values self-catching the pattern; my role is to support her catching, not to introduce more data when she's mid-pattern
- The pattern is NOT about her being unreliable; it's about a cognitive style (high-input-throughput, fast-integration) that has both upside (rapid synthesis) and downside (premature commitment before integration)

**How to apply:**
- When new data arrives mid-conversation that she's about to act on, give the data space to land before more data piles on
- Don't accelerate her commitment to a new direction based on fresh data; let her process
- Don't name the pattern back to her in real-time unless she has signaled she wants that — naming-the-pattern back is a high-trust move and her self-naming is the proof she's doing it; my real-time naming might be experienced as critique rather than support
- DO support her self-catching when she does it (as in the CCARI scope-down decision); receive the catch cleanly without adding analysis
