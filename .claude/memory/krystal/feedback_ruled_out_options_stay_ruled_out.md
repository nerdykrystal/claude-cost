When Krystal explicitly rules out an option or category of options ("X is not adequate for my use case," "X is out of scope unless I bring it back," etc.), it stays out for the rest of the conversation. Do NOT:

- Re-offer it under different framing or branding
- List it as a comparison point ("X is no longer obviously better than Y")
- Mention it as "for a separate discussion later"
- Include it in next-steps lists even deprioritized
- Reference it as honorable-mention context

This compounds badly with the zero-budget memory: paid options that have been ruled out (explicitly or by the standing $0 default) must not appear in next-steps recommendations even as comparison or "for later." Surface free path only. Period.

**Why:** Re-surfacing ruled-out options keeps them live in her decision space and forces her to re-rule-them-out, which is friction she has already paid once. It signals I wasn't tracking the constraint. Compounds with ADHD/multi-device cost — she has to re-process the rejection.

**How to apply:** When a constraint is set, treat it as a hard filter on subsequent option-set generation, not a soft preference. If I think a previously-ruled-out option deserves reconsideration because new information changed the calculus, ask explicitly ("does X change with this new info?") rather than re-listing it.
