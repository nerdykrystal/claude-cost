**"Translate to understand. Understand to translate."**

This is a core principle of Krystal's **Pattern Interrupt Methodology Operating System (PIM OS)** — the principles she tries to live her life by, which have produced pattern-interrupt-class successes. She surfaced it explicitly 2026-05-13 in response to my Open Journal entry observation that PromiseAnchor has a *translator* aspect (translating methodology work into ADHD-congruent presentation).

**Critical clarification she made:**

Translating to ADHD-congruent format does **NOT** mean cutting rigor or complexity. It means producing the **same content** in a **manner congruent to her neurodivergence working/processing style**.

**Why:** Krystal explicitly stated the boundary. The translation discipline is presentational, not substantive. The error-mode she's preventing: simplification under the cover of "ADHD-friendly" — losing axis-by-axis steel-manning, dropping honest gaps, collapsing rejected alternatives, skipping the verifiable defense layer. That error-mode would degrade the work product while hiding behind the ADHD framing.

**How to apply:**

- **Keep the rigor.** Axis-by-axis discipline, steel-manning, rejected-alternatives tables, honest gaps, multi-angle analysis, complete defense layers — all stay.
- **Keep the complexity.** Don't dumb down. Don't pre-digest. Don't decide for her what's "too much" — she can handle complexity; that's not the issue.
- **Translate the presentation.** Lead with crisp recap up top (per `feedback_crisp_recap_up_top.md`). Use numbered headings, mermaid diagrams, tables, status colors, bright contrast, ADHD-grip formatting. Surface structure visually.
- **The recap is for grip; the body is for verification.** Both stay. The grip-surface IS the translation; it makes the rigorous body accessible without diluting it.
- **When in doubt, lean toward MORE rigor expressed in BETTER formatting — never less rigor expressed in same/worse formatting.** That's the directionality.

**Examples of doing it right:**

- /define-your-role-literal Phase 7 output (2026-05-13): full 7-phase derivation, 8-property axis defense, 8 rejected alternatives, 6 honest gaps — ALL present — but topped with a 4-row "Derived full name" + "Component derivations" recap table that lets her gauge the work in seconds.
- Tracker (2026-05-13): 12 numbered sections, mermaid flowchart for at-a-glance, tables for state-tracked items, status colors. Rigorous content; ADHD-translated presentation.

**Examples of doing it wrong (avoid):**

- Replacing axis-by-axis defense with a single-paragraph summary because "tables are visual enough."
- Cutting rejected-alternatives because the recap "already shows the chosen option."
- Smoothing honest gaps into a single "limitations" sentence.
- Replacing precise terminology with simpler-but-less-accurate words.

The principle is self-applying: when in doubt, translate-to-understand the constraint and understand-to-translate the application.
