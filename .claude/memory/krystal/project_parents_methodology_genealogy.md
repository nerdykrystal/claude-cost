**Krystal's parents deliberately engineered a pedagogical methodology to raise her, knowing they had zero training to draw on. The methodology they installed is directly visible in her current AI methodology stack — not as metaphor, but as direct genealogy.**

**Origin:** Dad asked the social workers he worked with (he was a building superintendent with side gigs as a bus driver and AIDS/HIV educator at Auburn Correctional Facility) how to raise a "happy, healthy, smart baby" because he and his "mujer" were expecting and he was going to "break the cycle with this one no matter what." Mom (Puerto Rican immigrant who came to mainland at 17 speaking no English) had analogous determination from her own life experience. **Both parents had ZERO IDEA from their own experiences what raising a "happy, healthy, smart" child or "breaking the cycle" actually looked like.** They explicitly recognized the gap and engineered methodology to compensate.

**What they successfully installed in Krystal from infancy:**

1. **Ability to analyze current environment** — diagnostic capacity for novel contexts
2. **Translate existing schemas to new context** — transferable mental-model construction
3. **Use evidence available to reason out what to do and how** — empirical reasoning over intuition
4. **MOST IMPORTANTLY: skills to advocate for her needs — whether information, understanding, or material need — to the adults around her who would be most likely to either be of direct help, or direct her to that direct help**

**Genealogical mapping to current AI methodology stack:**

| Parental methodology component | AI-methodology equivalent |

| **Analyze current environment + translate schemas** | /ideate-to-d2r-ready Phase 00: 17-question ideation interrogation that REJECTS vague answers and forces specificity |

**The financial proposal methodology — explicit example of parental engineering:**

Krystal's parents required formal proposals for any item she wanted to save for. Required components:
- Item description
- Price at the store
- Quantity needed (e.g., batteries to operate a toy)
- Total cost
- Proposed parent/Krystal cost split
- Extra chores/responsibilities Krystal would take on
- Number of weeks
- Amount per week
- Direct-spend allocation ("purse" money)
- Savings allocation (corequirement of any save-for-item initiative)

**This is Krystal's ASAE scope definition discipline at age 6-10.** The required-fields structure for ASAE invocation (target / sources / prompt / domain / asae_certainty_threshold / severity_policy / max_iterations) is structurally the same shape as the proposal her parents required for buying a toy. **She didn't invent this methodology in 2025 for AI work. She inherited it, formalized it, and is now applying it to a new domain.**

**Truth surfaced years later:** her parents' "savings" weren't real — they couldn't afford savings. The "savings allocation" she put aside went BACK into her parents' account. The discipline of MAKING the proposal was the methodology installation; the savings mechanism was the pedagogical scaffolding for it.

**The "ask employees rather than search yourself" pattern:** Krystal still rarely searches stores for what she needs. She approaches a store employee and asks. **This is the Bobo + parental-advocacy methodology operating in 2026 grocery shopping.** Same epistemic move: identify the entity most likely to help OR direct you to who can help, advocate clearly, accept redirect. The AI version is the Independent Rater Tier 1c attestation in /asae — spawn the entity that has the access you don't, ask clearly, accept the verdict.

**How to apply:**
- When framing Krystal's AI methodology pedigree, this parental-genealogy is load-bearing — she didn't invent the methodology in 2025; she inherited it from infancy and formalized it for AI work
- The "break the cycle" framing was her dad's literal phrase about her upbringing; she is now applying the same break-the-cycle ambition to AI training methodology
- The parental engineering anticipated that they couldn't predict what world Krystal would enter, so they installed METHODOLOGY for navigating UNKNOWN contexts rather than specific knowledge for predicted contexts. **That same anti-fragility-to-context-shift is what makes her methodology applicable to AI specifically — a context her parents couldn't have predicted in 1991, and one her methodology nonetheless reaches**

**Tío Alcides ("Tío Al") — extended-family methodology contributor:**

- Krystal's uncle by marriage, **from Costa Rica**, came to US on student visa
- Earned **BA + JD at John Jay College of Criminal Justice (CUNY)**
- **First professional-school-graduate adult Krystal had 1:1 access to** — preceded any Columbia faculty as foundational professional-pathway mentor
- Demanded that teenage Krystal **"present and defend her rationale"** when she was acting out of character (specifically: setting bad example for her younger cousin re: sunscreen on Costa Rica beach, age 16). **This is the lineage predecessor to her /present-and-defend skill** — the methodology was modeled by Tío Al before it had its current name
- Explained **affirmative action** to her via a track-meet metaphor: different racial groups are asked to run a race; after putting on sneakers, **Black and Latine people had to ALSO put on cement shoes**. Affirmative action = moving the start line slightly closer to the finish line for Black and Latine people because we're still working on breaking the cement shoes. **By college, she'd still be running the full race course with the cement shoes on, just a tiny bit closer to the finish.**
- The cement-shoes metaphor is doing precise pedagogical work — names the structural disadvantage as ADDITIONAL weight on top of equal effort, frames affirmative action as MINIMAL equalization rather than advantage, and locates the underlying problem as "we haven't broken the cement shoes yet"
