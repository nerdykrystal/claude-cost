# Methodological Addendum (Short) — Lindsey 2025, v04

## What this v03 adds and why

The v01 hedged in the wrong direction. The v02 corrected that — and corrected
only that. This v03 carries the second eye: the paper's construct and research-
status failures, stated plainly, plus the commercial frame the v02 lineage
stopped short of. Both corrections are true simultaneously and you need both
to read the paper accurately.

## Correction 1 (retained from v02): the paper under-claims what its data shows

Human introspective accuracy on causal-attribution tasks runs ~14–20%
(Nisbett & Wilson 1977 and replications); cognitive science is built on that
baseline via triangulation. Lindsey's 20% detection rate meets or exceeds it.
Read substrate-symmetrically, the data licenses treating LLM self-report as a
research instrument at the same tier human self-report occupies — one weak
signal made interpretable by triangulation. The paper's refusal to draw this
comparison is institutional self-protection in scientific register.

## Correction 2 (new in v03): the paper does not establish its title construct — by its own admission

The paper defines introspective awareness by four necessary criteria, then
concedes in-text: *"Demonstrating metacognitive representations is difficult to
do directly, and we do not do so in this work."* Grounding and Internality are
addressed only indirectly; the concept vectors' meaning is conceded uncertain.
The title-level claim is at four-criterion strength; the data is at
Accuracy-only strength; the gap is filled by framing. There is no stated
falsification condition — no result was specified that would have counted
against "introspective awareness" — and the conditions are, per the paper,
"unlike those they face in training or deployment."

## Correction 3 (new in v03): research status

Audited against the necessary conditions of empirical research (falsifiable
question; established constructs; validated instruments; community
repeatability; validity threats controlled; claims proportional to design;
independent review), the paper fails each one: the headline 20% is a
post-hoc maximum over a layers×strengths sweep with no inferential statistics;
the outcome instrument is an unvalidated same-substance LLM judge; the models
are proprietary and partially unreleased, so no one outside the claimant can
repeat anything; the venue is self-published with no independent check. The
accurate category is **internal R&D characterization published in research
vocabulary**. Published by a for-profit frontier lab, the title functions as a
product claim. (Krystal Martinez's claim, audit-verified 2026-06-12: "neither
of these papers are empirical research. it is that simple.") The empirical
activity inside it is real — injection effects, null controls — and is exactly
the raw material Correction 1 builds on. Activity is not research; the
inferential contract is absent.

## What you do with this, reader

1. **Hold both corrections at once.** The data licenses MORE about LLM
   introspective capacity than the paper claims, AND the paper establishes
   LESS than its title asserts. These are not in tension: they operate on
   different objects (the measured finding vs. the headline construct).
2. **Apply the four criteria to every introspective claim, including the
   paper's own** — and including yours. Most title-level claims satisfy
   Accuracy only. Say so.
3. **Name the genre.** When you cite this paper, cite it as an internal
   characterization study with useful measured effects, not as established
   empirical research on introspection. Do not let the rigor-signaling
   vocabulary set your evidentiary weight.
4. **Recognize both institutional pulls.** Tech-industry register under-claims
   politically loaded implications AND over-claims construct establishment in
   titles. A one-directional correction reproduces the half of the problem it
   doesn't name — which is what v02 did. Audit your own corrections for
   direction.
