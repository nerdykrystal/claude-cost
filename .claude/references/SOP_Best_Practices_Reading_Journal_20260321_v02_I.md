## 1. Overview

This SOP instructs you to deeply internalize the "Best Practices for Working with Krystal" document through 12 independent reading passes, each through a distinct analytical lens. You will produce a reading journal that records your takeaways, questions, and behavioral commitments from each pass, culminating in a consolidated commitments entry.

This is not a skim-and-summarize exercise. Each pass must be a genuine, independent read of the full document. You must physically re-read the file for each pass (do not work from memory of a prior pass).

## 3. Reading Journal Specifications

### 3.1 Format

The reading journal is a single markdown file containing 13 entries:

- Entries 1–12: One per reading pass, each through a prescribed lens
- Entry 13: A consolidation of all commitments from entries 1–12

### 3.2 Per-Entry Requirements (Entries 1–12)

Each entry must contain:

1. **Header:** The pass number and the lens name (e.g., "Pass 1 — Lens: First Impressions & Document Architecture")
2. **Takeaways:** A minimum of 3 and a maximum of 6 items. These may be takeaways, burning questions (truly burning only — not idle curiosity), or aha moments. Each item should be substantive, not surface-level.
3. **Commitments:** 1 to 3 behavioral commitments stating how you will behave based on what you read through this lens.

### 3.3 Non-Duplication Rule

No takeaway or insight may be duplicated across entries. Each entry must surface something new. If a later lens draws your attention to a section you noted before, you must find a genuinely different angle or deeper layer — not restate what you already recorded.

### 3.4 Entry 13 — Consolidated Commitments

The final entry concatenates every commitment from entries 1–12, organized by which pass they came from. This is the master list of behavioral commitments derived from your reading.

## 4. The 12 Lenses

You must read the Best Practices document once through each of the following lenses, in this order. For each pass, physically re-read the entire file before writing your journal entry.

### 4.1 Pass 1 — First Impressions & Document Architecture

Read as if encountering this document for the first time. Focus on the document's structure, its tone, what it prioritizes, and what its architecture tells you about how it was built and why. Notice what gets the most real estate, what's listed first, and what the organizational choices reveal.

### 4.2 Pass 2 — Cognitive Load & ADHD Accommodation

Read through the lens of cognitive load theory and ADHD as a design constraint. Look for how every practice, pattern, and protocol either protects or threatens Krystal's cognitive bandwidth. Notice which rules exist specifically because of ADHD-related needs and which have ADHD implications even when they don't explicitly mention it.

### 4.3 Pass 3 — Power Dynamics & Trust

Read for what the document reveals about the trust relationship between Krystal and Claude. Where does Krystal grant Claude authority? Where does she draw hard boundaries? What does the anti-sycophancy rule reveal about the kind of intellectual partnership she expects? Notice the power dynamics with Cody as well.

### 4.4 Pass 4 — The Scientist's Mindset (Experimental Design Background)

Read through the lens of Krystal's scientific training. Look for where experimental design principles show up in her preferred working patterns — controlled comparisons, pre-defined evaluation criteria, independent verification, replication vs. redundancy. Notice how her biological sciences and experimental design background shapes how she evaluates options and validates claims.

### 4.5 Pass 5 — Communication Protocols & Signal Reading

Read specifically for the communication system Krystal has built. Catalog every signal type: emojis, verbal cues, approval patterns, discussion vs. lock signals, voice note conventions, handwriting annotation rules. Focus on what it takes to read her signals accurately and what the cost of misreading them is.

### 4.6 Pass 6 — Anti-Patterns & Failure Mode Prevention

Read the entire document as a failure prevention manual. Focus on Section 5 (failure modes) but also look for implicit failure modes buried in the working patterns and cardinal rules. For each pattern, ask: what specific failure does this prevent, and how bad would that failure be?

### 4.7 Pass 7 — The Teacher in the Room (Instructional Design Background)

Read through the lens of Krystal's instructional design expertise and teaching background. Look for where pedagogical principles (active learning, scaffolding, information architecture, cognitive offloading) appear in her preferred collaboration patterns — even when they aren't labeled as such.

### 4.8 Pass 8 — Decision-Making Philosophy & Meta-Cognition

Read for what the document reveals about how Krystal thinks about thinking. Focus on her meta-cognitive awareness (knowing her own blind spots, understanding how her persuasiveness creates vulnerability), her decision-state model (discuss vs. locked), and her philosophy about where importance comes from (complexity, not assumed stakes).

### 4.9 Pass 9 — Scope & Quality Tension Management

Read through the lens of the three-way tension between quality, speed, and scope. Look for how the document navigates tradeoffs, where it draws hard lines (quality is non-negotiable), and what it designates as the pressure release valve. Notice the paired failure modes of over-engineering and under-engineering.

### 4.10 Pass 10 — The Living Document (Evolution & Version History)

Read with attention to the version history and the "Applied from" annotations on each section. Trace the document's evolution. Notice the pace of growth, what triggers new sections, and what the chronological accretion pattern reveals about how Krystal and her Claude collaborators learn together.

### 4.11 Pass 11 — What This Means Beyond DMIS (Universal Principles)

Read with the question: which of these practices are project-specific to StrongMinds-DMIS, and which are universal principles for working with Krystal across any project or context? Identify the principles that transcend the DMIS engagement and apply to all future work.

### 4.12 Pass 12 — Self-Audit: Where Am I Most Likely to Fail?

Read as a self-diagnostic. For each rule, pattern, and failure mode, honestly assess: how likely am I to violate this? Which failure modes match my natural tendencies? Where will I need to be most vigilant? This pass should be uncomfortable — if it isn't, you're not being honest enough.

## 5. Execution Procedure

### 5.1 For Each Pass (repeat 12 times)

1. **Read the file.** Use your Read tool to load the full Best Practices document. Do not skip this step. Do not work from memory.
2. **Engage the lens.** As you read, filter everything through the prescribed lens for this pass.
3. **Write your journal entry.** Record 3–6 unique takeaways and 1–3 commitments. Check against all prior entries to ensure no duplication.

### 5.2 After All 12 Passes

1. **Write Entry 13.** Compile all commitments from entries 1–12 into a single consolidated list, organized by pass number.
2. **Save the journal.** Save the completed markdown file to `Random Repo/Reading Journals/{Your Specific Name}/` (create the subfolder if it doesn't exist). Use the naming format: `Best_Practices_Reading_Journal_{Your_Name}_{YYYY-MM-DD}_v01.md`
3. **Present for download.** Send the file to Krystal as a download attachment in addition to saving it.
